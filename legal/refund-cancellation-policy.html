Refund and Cancellation Policy

Version: AGS-RCP-2026-07-16-v1
Effective date: July 16, 2026

This Policy applies to Aurevanta Global Solutions service requests, projects, packages, add-ons, retainers, subscriptions, reservations, and custom work. It is incorporated into the Client Service Agreement.

1. Estimates and Final Authorization

Website prices and calculators are estimates unless expressly identified as fixed and accepted by Aurevanta. A project becomes authorized only after scope confirmation, acceptance of applicable terms, and any required payment or deposit. Aurevanta may revise an estimate when the client changes the scope or when new facts materially affect the work.

2. Deposits and Reservation Fees

Deposits reserve production capacity and authorize Aurevanta to begin preparation, scheduling, research, administration, purchasing, or production. Once work begins or capacity has been reserved, a deposit is nonrefundable to the fullest extent permitted by law, except for any portion Aurevanta agrees in writing to refund or that applicable law requires to be refunded.

3. Cancellation Before Work Begins

If the client cancels before Aurevanta begins work and before noncancelable costs or capacity commitments are incurred, Aurevanta may refund the amount paid minus payment-processing charges, approved expenses, consultation fees, administrative setup, and any stated reservation fee. The timing and amount of any refund depend on the written scope and actual commitments.

4. Cancellation After Work Begins

If the client cancels after work begins, the client owes for work performed, work in progress, reserved capacity, approved expenses, third-party charges, noncancelable commitments, and applicable cancellation or closeout fees. Aurevanta may provide completed work in its then-current form after all amounts due are paid. Drafts, editable files, source files, credentials, licenses, and ownership rights may be withheld until full payment.

5. Custom and Digital Services

Custom strategy, consulting, design, branding, writing, development, administrative setup, digital products, media preparation, automation, and other personalized services are generally not returnable like physical retail products. Refunds are not available merely because the client changes preference, direction, leadership, budget, schedule, vendor, or business plans after work begins.

6. Subscription and Retainer Cancellation

Monthly or annual services continue until cancelled under the applicable plan. Unless the signed scope provides otherwise, cancellation prevents future renewal but does not retroactively refund a completed or current billing period. To avoid the next renewal, cancellation should be received at least seven calendar days before the renewal date. Annual plans may be priced at a discount in exchange for the annual commitment; early cancellation does not automatically convert the plan into a month-to-month arrangement or create a prorated refund.

7. Paused or Dormant Projects

Aurevanta may pause a project when required information, approvals, access, feedback, or payment is overdue. If a project remains inactive because of the client for thirty calendar days, Aurevanta may close or archive it, release reserved capacity, and require a restart fee, updated estimate, and new schedule before resuming. Fees already earned remain due.

8. Scope Changes and Revisions

Additional concepts, deliverables, pages, formats, meetings, stakeholders, revisions, rush work, reshoots, travel, integrations, or changed instructions may create additional charges. The client may decline the additional work, but amounts already earned under the original scope remain due.

9. Rescheduling

On-location, media, photography, travel, meeting, and production dates may be rescheduled because of safety, weather, illness, access, regulatory restrictions, equipment issues, or other reasonable operational concerns. Client-requested rescheduling may require reimbursement of nonrefundable expenses and a rescheduling fee when capacity or vendors were committed.

10. Aurevanta Cancellation

If Aurevanta cancels an accepted project for reasons not caused by the client and cannot provide a reasonable alternative, Aurevanta will refund unearned fees for work not performed. Aurevanta may retain fees for completed work, approved expenses, deliverables already supplied, and commitments already incurred. Aurevanta may terminate without refund of earned fees when the termination results from client breach, nonpayment, abusive conduct, unlawful requests, safety risk, security concerns, infringement concerns, or repeated failure to cooperate.

11. Limited Remedy for Nonconforming Work

If a paid deliverable materially fails to conform to the written scope, the client must provide specific written notice within fourteen calendar days after delivery. Aurevanta may reasonably correct, replace, re-perform, or issue a proportionate refund for the affected materially nonconforming portion. This remedy does not cover changed preference, new requirements, third-party platform changes, client modifications, client-supplied errors, or use beyond the intended purpose.

12. Chargebacks and Payment Disputes

The client should contact Aurevanta promptly before initiating a payment dispute so the parties can review the scope, records, and available remedy. A chargeback does not eliminate the underlying contractual payment obligation. Aurevanta may suspend services, revoke access to unpaid deliverables where lawful, and submit agreement, consent, communication, delivery, and usage records to the payment provider. Nothing in this section limits rights that applicable law or card-network rules do not permit the client to waive.

13. Processing Time

Approved refunds are returned through the original payment method when reasonably possible. Banks and payment providers control final posting times and may retain their own processing fees.

14. Nonwaivable Rights

This Policy applies only to the fullest extent permitted by law. It does not exclude refund, cancellation, cooling-off, warranty, or consumer rights that applicable law makes nonwaivable.

Before purchase: The final order summary should identify the amount due, whether it is a deposit or full payment, what work begins upon payment, the renewal interval if any, and any package-specific cancellation terms.
