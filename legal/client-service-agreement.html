Client Service Agreement

Version: AGS-CSA-2026-07-16-v1
Effective date: July 16, 2026
Provider: Aurevanta Global Solutions and its owner, personnel, contractors, and authorized service providers (“Aurevanta”).
Important: This Agreement governs service requests, estimates, projects, subscriptions, and related deliverables unless a later signed statement of work or written agreement states otherwise. A specific statement of work controls only where it expressly conflicts with this Agreement.

1. Electronic Acceptance and Authority

By checking the acceptance box, typing a name, submitting a request, signing a statement of work, or making payment, the client agrees to this Agreement electronically. The signer represents that the signer is at least eighteen years old and has authority to bind the named individual, business, nonprofit, ministry, or organization.

2. Request Review; No Automatic Acceptance

A website submission, displayed price, automated estimate, consultation, or payment link does not require Aurevanta to accept a project. Aurevanta may conduct conflict, capacity, safety, legal, ethical, technical, credit, and scope review before accepting work. Aurevanta may lawfully decline, pause, limit, or terminate a request or project, including for nonpayment, abusive conduct, unlawful or unsafe instructions, conflicts, missing information, repeated scope expansion, reputational risk, technical infeasibility, or lack of capacity.

3. Scope, Deliverables, and Change Orders

Services are limited to the written package, estimate, order summary, or statement of work accepted by Aurevanta. Anything not expressly included is excluded. New requests, expanded objectives, additional pages, additional concepts, extra revisions, new formats, accelerated deadlines, changed source materials, or changed instructions may require a written change order and additional fees. Aurevanta is not required to begin changed work until the client approves the revised scope, schedule, and price.

4. Independent Contractor; Methods and Non-Exclusivity

Aurevanta is an independent contractor and retains control over the professional methods, tools, sequence, schedule, workflow, work location, and personnel used to perform the services, subject to the agreed deliverables and deadlines. Nothing creates employment, partnership, fiduciary, joint-venture, franchise, or agency status. Unless a signed statement of work says otherwise, Aurevanta may serve other clients, including clients in similar industries, and the engagement is non-exclusive.

5. Personnel, Subcontractors, Platforms, and AI-Assisted Tools

Aurevanta may use qualified employees, independent contractors, specialists, cloud services, automation platforms, and artificial-intelligence-assisted tools to support research, drafting, design, administration, analysis, production, and quality control. Aurevanta remains responsible for managing the agreed service. The client authorizes necessary processing of project information through selected service providers, subject to the Privacy Policy. Aurevanta will use reasonable human review but does not represent that every deliverable is produced entirely without automated assistance.

6. Client Responsibilities

The client must provide accurate, complete, lawful, and timely information, feedback, approvals, credentials, content, files, access, and decisions. The client is responsible for appointing one authorized decision-maker and for resolving conflicting instructions among its personnel. Delays caused by the client, third parties, platform outages, access restrictions, or missing materials extend timelines and may require rescheduling or restart fees.

7. Client Materials, Permissions, and Upload Warranty

The client represents that it owns or has valid permission to use and provide every name, logo, trademark, image, video, recording, document, dataset, testimonial, likeness, location, design, text, account, credential, and other material supplied to Aurevanta. The client authorizes Aurevanta to copy, edit, transform, transmit, store, display, and use those materials as reasonably necessary to perform the project. The client must not upload payment-card data, passwords, Social Security numbers, protected health information, classified information, unlawfully obtained data, malware, or other highly sensitive information unless Aurevanta expressly approves a secure method in writing.

8. Media, Photography, Drone, and Location Responsibilities

For photography, video, interview, event, or drone-related services, the client is responsible for securing required owner permissions, participant releases, venue permissions, permits, access, safety coordination, and notice to affected persons unless the written scope assigns a specific responsibility to Aurevanta. Operations remain subject to weather, safety, access, equipment, airspace, regulatory, and location conditions. Aurevanta may stop or reschedule any activity it reasonably considers unsafe, unlawful, disruptive, or impracticable.

9. Fees, Deposits, Subscriptions, and Taxes

Prices are stated in United States dollars unless otherwise written. Estimates are based on information available when issued and may change after scope review. One-time projects may require a deposit before scheduling and full payment before final editable files, credentials, licenses, or ownership rights are released. Subscription services renew at the selected interval until cancelled under the applicable cancellation terms. The client is responsible for applicable taxes, duties, bank fees, currency-conversion fees, and third-party charges not expressly included.

10. Cancellation, Suspension, Refunds, and Charge Disputes

The Refund and Cancellation Policy is incorporated into this Agreement. Fees for completed work, reserved capacity, approved third-party expenses, custom production, and work already performed are nonrefundable to the fullest extent permitted by law. Aurevanta may suspend services and withhold unfinished or final deliverables while an undisputed balance remains unpaid. Nothing in this Agreement limits rights that applicable law does not allow the client to waive.

11. Revisions, Review, and Acceptance

Revision limits are determined by the selected package or statement of work. A revision means a reasonable modification to an existing approved direction, not a new concept or changed scope. The client must review materials promptly and provide consolidated feedback. Aurevanta may pause a project when feedback is late. Unless a written scope provides another period, final deliverables are deemed accepted fourteen calendar days after delivery if the client has not provided a written description of a material failure to conform to the agreed scope.

12. Timeline and Force Majeure

Dates are estimates unless expressly identified as guaranteed in a signed statement of work. Aurevanta is not responsible for delay or failure caused by circumstances beyond reasonable control, including illness, emergency, disaster, severe weather, internet or platform outage, government action, labor disruption, supply interruption, cyber incident, war, civil disorder, travel restriction, equipment failure despite reasonable care, or third-party delay. Aurevanta may adjust scheduling and delivery methods when reasonably necessary.

13. Intellectual Property

Each party retains ownership of materials, trademarks, systems, processes, templates, know-how, software, libraries, methods, and intellectual property owned or developed independently of the project. Unless the written scope states otherwise, ownership of specifically commissioned final deliverables transfers to the client only after Aurevanta receives full payment, excluding Aurevanta’s pre-existing materials, reusable systems, working methods, source libraries, licensed assets, drafts, rejected concepts, and third-party materials. To the extent Aurevanta material is embedded in a paid final deliverable, Aurevanta grants the client a non-exclusive license reasonably necessary to use that final deliverable for its intended purpose. Third-party materials remain subject to their own licenses.

14. Portfolio and Publicity

Aurevanta will request separate optional permission before publicly identifying the client or displaying nonpublic project materials as a portfolio item, testimonial, or case study. Refusal of portfolio permission does not prevent purchase of services. Aurevanta may still identify and preserve project records privately for administration, legal compliance, security, quality control, and dispute resolution.

15. Confidentiality

Each party will use reasonable care to protect confidential nonpublic information received from the other and will use it for the engagement and legitimate administrative purposes. Confidentiality does not apply to information that is public without breach, already lawfully known, independently developed, properly received from another source, approved for release, or required to be disclosed by law. Aurevanta may share necessary information with personnel and service providers who are supporting the engagement.

16. No Legal, Tax, Accounting, Investment, Insurance, or Licensed Advice

Aurevanta provides consulting, administrative preparation, creative, technology, organizational-development, and compliance-readiness support. Unless a separately identified licensed professional is expressly engaged, Aurevanta does not provide legal advice, legal representation, tax advice, certified public accounting, investment advice, insurance advice, regulated filing approval, or professional licensing opinions. Templates and drafts require the client’s independent review and, when appropriate, review by qualified licensed counsel or other professionals.

17. No Guaranteed Outcome

Aurevanta does not guarantee revenue, customers, donors, grants, funding, rankings, media performance, platform approval, nonprofit approval, regulatory approval, legal compliance, audience growth, conversion rate, business success, or any particular commercial result. Results depend on many factors beyond Aurevanta’s control. Aurevanta promises only to perform accepted services in a professional manner substantially consistent with the written scope.

18. Limited Correction Warranty

If a paid final deliverable materially fails to conform to the written scope and the client gives detailed written notice within fourteen calendar days after delivery, Aurevanta’s obligation is limited, at its option, to reasonable correction, replacement, re-performance, or a proportionate refund for the affected undelivered or materially nonconforming portion. This limited remedy does not cover changed preferences, third-party changes, client edits, misuse, new requirements, platform policy changes, or defects caused by client materials or instructions.

19. Disclaimer and Limitation of Liability

Except for the express limited warranty above and to the fullest extent permitted by law, services and deliverables are provided “as is” and “as available,” without implied warranties of merchantability, fitness for a particular purpose, title, noninfringement, uninterrupted operation, or error-free performance. To the fullest extent permitted by law, Aurevanta will not be liable for indirect, incidental, special, exemplary, punitive, or consequential damages, lost profits, lost opportunities, lost data, lost goodwill, or third-party platform actions. Aurevanta’s aggregate liability arising from a particular project will not exceed the fees actually paid to Aurevanta for the specific affected service giving rise to the claim. These limits do not apply where applicable law prohibits the limitation.

20. Client Indemnity

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the client will defend, indemnify, and hold Aurevanta and its personnel harmless from third-party claims, damages, penalties, costs, and reasonable legal fees arising from client-supplied materials, unlawful instructions, inaccurate representations, failure to obtain permissions, infringement by client content, misuse of deliverables, operation of the client’s business, or breach of this Agreement. This obligation does not cover claims caused by Aurevanta’s fraud, willful misconduct, or liability that law does not permit to be shifted.

21. Communications

The client authorizes transactional communications reasonably necessary to review, administer, perform, bill, secure, and complete the engagement. Marketing communications require separate optional consent and may be withdrawn. The client must maintain accurate contact information and monitor the designated email address.

22. Honest Reviews and Protected Rights

Nothing in this Agreement prevents a client from submitting an honest review, communicating with government agencies, reporting suspected unlawful conduct, participating in legal proceedings, or exercising rights that cannot lawfully be waived. The client may not knowingly make false statements, disclose protected confidential information, infringe intellectual property, impersonate another person, or engage in unlawful harassment.

23. Dispute Resolution and Governing Law

Before filing a non-emergency claim, the parties will attempt in good faith for at least thirty days to resolve the dispute through written notice and direct discussion. Unless a signed statement of work validly provides otherwise, this Agreement is governed by Michigan law, without regard to conflict-of-law rules, and the parties consent to personal jurisdiction and venue in state or federal courts located in or serving Wayne County, Michigan. Either party may seek urgent injunctive or equitable relief where reasonably necessary to protect confidential information, intellectual property, systems, safety, or access rights.

24. Termination

Either party may terminate future unperformed services by written notice, subject to payment for completed work, work in progress, reserved capacity, approved expenses, noncancelable commitments, and applicable cancellation fees. Aurevanta may immediately suspend or terminate for material breach, nonpayment, abusive conduct, unlawful or unsafe requests, security risk, infringement concerns, or repeated failure to cooperate. Provisions concerning payment, intellectual property, confidentiality, disclaimers, liability, indemnity, disputes, and records survive termination.

25. Entire Agreement; Severability; Assignment

This Agreement, the accepted order summary, Privacy Policy, Refund and Cancellation Policy, applicable media authorization, and any signed statement of work form the entire agreement concerning the service. If a provision is unenforceable, it will be modified only as much as necessary and the remainder remains effective. The client may not assign the engagement without Aurevanta’s written consent. Aurevanta may assign the agreement as part of a lawful reorganization, sale, merger, or transfer of the business and may delegate performance to authorized service providers.

26. Records and Versions

The version accepted at submission or payment applies to that transaction unless the parties later agree otherwise. Electronic records may include the accepted document version, selected package, service configuration, displayed total, typed name, authority statement, date and time, email address, browser information, internet address, payment record, and related audit data. The client should download or print a copy for its records.
