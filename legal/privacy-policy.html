Privacy Policy

Version: AGS-PRIV-2026-07-16-v1
Effective date: July 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how Aurevanta Global Solutions (“Aurevanta,” “we,” “us,” or “our”) collects, uses, stores, discloses, and protects information submitted through our website, service-request process, communications, files, projects, and related systems.

1. Information We May Collect

  • Identity and contact information, including name, email, telephone number, mailing address, country, and time zone.
  • Business and organization information, including business name, entity type, role, industry, website, social links, and operational details.
  • Project information, including selected packages, requested services, objectives, budget, timeline, references, feedback, and approvals.
  • Files and content, including logos, images, videos, audio, documents, spreadsheets, presentations, policies, business records, and other project materials.
  • Transaction and billing information, including invoice status, selected billing plan, payment confirmation, and limited transaction metadata. Payment-card details should be collected and processed by the payment provider, not stored by this website.
  • Technical and audit information, including internet address, browser type, device information, timestamps, referral pages, consent records, security events, and system logs.
  • Communications, including emails, messages, consultation notes, customer-service inquiries, and project correspondence.

2. Why We Use Information

  • To review, quote, accept, administer, perform, secure, and complete service requests.
  • To calculate estimates, prepare scopes of work, create invoices, process payments, and maintain transaction records.
  • To communicate about requests, deadlines, approvals, missing information, support, and project status.
  • To store and process files needed for requested services.
  • To detect fraud, abuse, malware, security risks, prohibited activity, and violations of our agreements.
  • To improve our services, documentation, workflows, quality control, and customer experience.
  • To comply with legal, accounting, tax, regulatory, insurance, audit, and dispute-resolution obligations.
  • To send marketing communications only where permitted and subject to separate consent or applicable opt-out rights.

3. Legal Bases and Consent

Depending on the location and circumstances, we may process information because it is necessary to respond to a request, perform or prepare a contract, comply with law, protect legitimate business and security interests, establish or defend legal claims, or because the individual has given consent. Optional consent, such as consent to marketing or public portfolio use, is requested separately and may be withdrawn for future use.

4. Service Providers and Disclosures

We may disclose information to personnel and service providers that support hosting, database services, secure storage, payment processing, email delivery, customer relationship management, analytics, automation, design, production, legal review, accounting, insurance, security, and project fulfillment. They receive only information reasonably necessary for their function and may be subject to their own privacy terms. We may also disclose information when required by law, court order, lawful process, safety necessity, fraud investigation, business transfer, or to enforce agreements and protect rights.

5. AI-Assisted Processing

We may use artificial-intelligence-assisted tools to support drafting, summarization, design exploration, classification, research, workflow automation, and quality review. We use reasonable judgment when selecting what information to process. Clients should not submit highly sensitive or regulated information unless a secure method and intended processing have been agreed in writing.

6. Uploaded Files

Files are used to evaluate and perform requested services and for related administration, security, backup, quality control, and dispute resolution. The client must have authority to provide the files. Do not upload passwords, full payment-card data, Social Security numbers, protected health information, classified information, malware, or unlawfully obtained personal information without prior written approval and a designated secure method.

7. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as reasonably necessary for the project, customer relationship, legal obligations, accounting, tax, security, warranty, backup, records management, and dispute resolution. Retention periods may vary by record type. When information is no longer reasonably required, we may delete, anonymize, or securely archive it, subject to legal and technical limitations.

8. Security

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards appropriate to the nature of the information and available systems. No internet transmission, cloud platform, storage system, or security measure can guarantee absolute security. Clients are responsible for using secure devices, protecting credentials, and promptly reporting suspected compromise.

9. International Processing

Aurevanta may work with clients and service providers in multiple countries. Information may be processed in the United States and other locations where our personnel or service providers operate. Applicable safeguards and legal requirements may differ by jurisdiction.

10. Privacy Choices and Requests

Subject to applicable law, individuals may request access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, or information about use and disclosure. They may also withdraw optional consent for future processing and unsubscribe from marketing. Some information may be retained where required for contract performance, legal compliance, fraud prevention, accounting, security, or legal claims. Requests should be sent through the contact method published on the website and must include enough information to verify identity and locate the relevant records.

11. California and Other U.S. State Rights

If a state privacy law applies to Aurevanta and the requesting person, additional rights may include rights to know, access, correct, delete, obtain a copy, and opt out of certain sales, sharing, targeted advertising, or profiling. Aurevanta does not intend to sell personal information for money. If our practices change or applicable law requires additional links or processes, we will update this Policy and the website.

12. European Economic Area, United Kingdom, and Similar Jurisdictions

Where applicable, individuals may have rights to access, correct, erase, restrict, object, transfer information, withdraw consent, and complain to a supervisory authority. Consent is not used as a condition for processing that is unnecessary to provide a requested service. Withdrawal of consent does not invalidate processing lawfully completed before withdrawal.

13. Cookies and Analytics

The website may use necessary storage, security tools, and limited analytics. If nonessential advertising, tracking, or analytics cookies are introduced, Aurevanta should deploy an appropriate notice and consent mechanism where required. Browser controls may also limit cookies or local storage.

14. Children

Our services are intended for adults and authorized representatives of businesses and organizations. We do not knowingly solicit service orders directly from children. A parent, guardian, school, nonprofit, or other authorized adult must manage any project involving minors and secure required permissions and releases.

15. Third-Party Links and Platforms

Our website may link to payment processors, forms, scheduling systems, cloud storage, social media, and other third-party platforms. Their privacy practices are governed by their own policies. Aurevanta is not responsible for the independent privacy or security practices of unaffiliated platforms.

16. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Policy to reflect new systems, services, legal requirements, or practices. The version accepted or provided for a particular transaction remains part of that transaction’s electronic record where required. Material changes will be posted with a revised effective date.

17. Contact

Privacy questions and requests should be submitted through the Aurevanta contact channel identified on the website. A dedicated privacy email and business mailing address should be added before public launch.

Separate choices: Necessary project communications, optional marketing, and optional portfolio or case-study permission are treated as distinct choices. Optional choices should not be preselected or required to purchase.
