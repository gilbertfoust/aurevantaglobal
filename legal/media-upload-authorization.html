Media and Upload Authorization

Version: AGS-MUA-2026-07-16-v1
Effective date: July 16, 2026

This Authorization supplements the Client Service Agreement when a client uploads files, supplies content, requests design or media work, authorizes photography or recording, or requests services involving property, locations, people, aircraft, drones, interviews, events, or public distribution.

1. Authority to Provide Materials

The client represents that it owns or has sufficient rights, licenses, consents, and authority to provide and authorize use of every uploaded or supplied item, including logos, trademarks, business names, photographs, recordings, voices, likenesses, documents, text, graphics, music, videos, testimonials, datasets, designs, and confidential records.

2. Limited Project License

The client grants Aurevanta and its authorized personnel and service providers a limited, non-exclusive license to receive, store, copy, crop, edit, transform, reproduce, transmit, display, combine, adapt, and otherwise use supplied materials only as reasonably necessary to evaluate, administer, perform, secure, back up, quality-check, deliver, and document the requested services.

3. Prohibited Uploads

The client must not upload or transmit malware, unlawfully obtained information, infringing content, defamatory material, content that exploits or endangers a person, passwords, authentication secrets, full payment-card information, Social Security numbers, protected health information, classified information, export-controlled technical data, or other highly sensitive material unless Aurevanta has expressly approved a designated secure process in writing.

4. Accuracy and Instructions

The client is responsible for checking names, numbers, dates, claims, translations, quotations, legal notices, contact details, product information, pricing, regulatory statements, and factual content before approval or publication. Aurevanta may identify apparent issues but is not the client’s legal, regulatory, tax, medical, engineering, or factual certifier.

5. Releases for People and Likenesses

The client is responsible for obtaining legally sufficient permission from people whose name, image, likeness, voice, performance, testimonial, personal information, or property appears in client-supplied or client-directed content, including parental or guardian authorization where minors are involved. Unless the written scope assigns this duty to Aurevanta, the client must retain those releases and provide copies upon reasonable request.

6. Property, Venue, Event, and Location Permissions

The client is responsible for obtaining permission to enter, photograph, record, stage, operate equipment at, or publish content depicting private property, venues, workplaces, events, controlled locations, copyrighted displays, artworks, signage, or protected interiors. The client must disclose location rules and known hazards before production.

7. Drone and Aerial Work

Drone or aerial work is subject to pilot availability, airspace, weather, location access, safety, equipment, insurance, privacy, and regulatory requirements. No requested shot or schedule overrides safety or applicable law. Aurevanta or its pilot may modify, postpone, relocate, or cancel an operation when conditions are unsuitable. The client remains responsible for site access and property authorization unless the scope states otherwise.

8. Live Events and Uncontrolled Conditions

For live events, interviews, photography, and field production, Aurevanta cannot guarantee the conduct, attendance, appearance, audio environment, lighting, weather, access, cooperation, or availability of subjects and third parties. Missed or unusable material caused by late access, participant refusal, interference, unsafe conditions, inaccurate schedules, venue restrictions, or events outside Aurevanta’s reasonable control may require rescheduling or additional fees.

9. Editing and Creative Discretion

Subject to the written scope and approved direction, the client authorizes reasonable creative and technical decisions regarding composition, editing, sequencing, color, formatting, compression, retouching, sound, pacing, layout, and production methods. Material changes requested after approval may be treated as additional scope.

10. Third-Party Assets and Licenses

Stock images, music, fonts, templates, software, footage, plugins, platform assets, and other third-party materials may be governed by separate licenses, attribution requirements, usage limits, territories, media restrictions, seat limits, or renewal fees. Aurevanta transfers only the rights it is legally permitted to transfer. The client is responsible for complying with disclosed third-party license conditions.

11. Sensitive and Confidential Content

The client must identify confidential or embargoed materials and should provide only the minimum information necessary. Aurevanta may refuse materials that create unreasonable security, legal, safety, privacy, or storage risk. Ordinary website upload tools should not be treated as suitable for regulated or highly sensitive information.

12. Public Portfolio Permission Is Separate

Permission to perform the project does not automatically authorize Aurevanta to publicly display confidential client materials as a portfolio item, case study, testimonial, or advertisement. Public portfolio use requires a separate optional affirmative choice or later written permission. Refusing portfolio permission does not affect the client’s ability to request or purchase services.

13. Removal and Retention

Uploaded files may be retained for active work, backups, legal records, accounting, security, quality control, warranty, and dispute resolution. Subject to applicable law and legitimate retention needs, a client may request deletion of unnecessary personal information or project files. Deletion from active systems may not immediately remove encrypted backups or records legally required to be retained.

14. Client Indemnity for Supplied Materials

To the fullest extent permitted by law, the client will defend, indemnify, and hold Aurevanta and its personnel harmless from third-party claims, damages, penalties, costs, and reasonable legal fees arising from client-supplied materials, client-directed publication, missing releases or permissions, infringement, unlawful data, inaccurate representations, or misuse of delivered media, except to the extent caused by Aurevanta’s fraud, willful misconduct, or liability that law does not permit to be shifted.

15. Electronic Acceptance

Checking the applicable authorization box, typing a name, uploading files, and submitting the request confirms that the client has read this Authorization and intends to be bound electronically. The accepted version, timestamp, signer, request details, file metadata, and related audit information should be preserved with the project record.

Media-specific requirement: The website should require this authorization whenever a customer selects the Media Package, corporate photography, drone photography, video production, interview, event, or on-location service.
